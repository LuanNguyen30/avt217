Restaurant Critique

PHO

When entering Pho’s main webpage, it presents you with its hero picture, a variety of dishes scattered across a wooden table. Its font sets a playful tone that would echo throughout the website. As the viewer scrolls down, the background image changes into an illustration of a contemporary place. Each picture is constant in style and color, setting the tone for the entire website and subsequently, the restaurant. I feel like the website leans towards the presentation of the website and not the restaurant’s food. I would improve if there were more pictures of the product they’re advertising.

___________________________________________________________________________

Green Rebel

Green Rebel is all about the quality of its ingredients, more specifically, it strives to provide its patrons organic products. Its purpose is extremely clear when you first open the website. The first words that catch the viewer’s eyes are “Don’t eat less. Just eat real.” It’s obviously implying that their products are GMO-free and are not unethically mass-produced. As the viewer scrolls down, a recurring theme of cardboard is apparent. This shows the viewer that the company is environmentally conscious. In fact, they have a whole page dedicated to what they’re doing to lessen environmental harm. The intentions and social views of this company are apart of the website’s design.

Chasin Tails

At first glance, the name of the restaurant doesn’t reveal much about the types of food it sells, but after loading the website and looking at its logo, it is very apparent what type of food this restaurant specializes in. The hero image establishes the restaurant menu, filling it with seafood ranging from shrimps, craps, to crawfish. Furthermore, the name of the restaurant has a  nautical twist embed into the “T” in tails. Instead of a “T,” it’s replaced with an anchor. What caught my attention is not the website’s immediate declaration of its purpose (to serve patrons seafood), but rather, its concurrency. It’s updated to adhere to the current state of the world. From an objective point of view, the website has a problem to solve: If people can’t leave their house, how do we get our food to them? The answer to this problem is the first thing present when a viewer loads the website, making the user experience more convenient. Additionally, the banner changes to the most current holiday. Currently, they have a banner dedicated to the Lunar New Year.

Five Guys

Five Guys has the same energy as Chasin Tails but takes it a step further. Their adaptation to the current event is evident, but they add two things Chasin Tails fail to make apparent: authority and the care for its customer. Authority is the main information shown when loading into the website. “Since 1986” is telling its patrons to trust the business. The number of decades they’ve spent in operation gives assurance to the customer about the experience and consistency of the product. Five Guys adds another level of reassurance through its covid-19 safety measures page. It gives patrons a simple but comprehensive list of all of the safety precautions they’re enforcing in order to keep their business safe and clean. This alone will encourage people who are wary about ordering from Five Guys, assuring them that their food will be handled safely.

Caffe Marchio

Caffe Marchio sells its product using a minimalistic strategy. It does not bog the viewer with walls of texts or complicated navigation, instead, it uses the products to sell itself. There are 5 images that make up the website’s banner and each of them displays its product in the most elegant way. Additionally, it gives the viewer a taste of what they’re selling, a variety of baked goods with drinks. Although it seems the first page is rather lackluster, the viewer can easily navigate through the website if they seek more information.
